Network Information Security Management - Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and analyse security risks and vulnerabilities in IT network systems and determine appropriate methodologies, tools and techniques to manage and/or solve them.

  • Design and critically appraise computer programs and systems to produce solutions that help manage and audit risk and security issues.

  • Gather and synthesise information from multiple sources (including internet security alerts & warning sites) to aid in the systematic analysis of security breaches and issues.

  • Articulate the legal, social, ethical and professional issues faced by information security professionals.

    • Reflection

    Seminar 2 took place this week and I made a presentation on our results from the team 3 scanning activities. The scan results were also shared on the Collaborative Discussion 2 forum.

    The feedback from the tutor was encouraging and it also provided insights on how to tackle the week 6 and 11 projects. The tutor advised exploring beyond the basic network commands and looking into more advanced penetration testing tools. I plan to spend the next couple of days reading about popular tools such as Kali Linux.

    TCP/IP vs ISO/OSI discussion The Week 3 lecture cast titled Network Fundamentals gave a good insight into the TCP/IP protocols and the OSI layers. To answer the question: “Would the Internet we have today be much better if it was based on the ISO/ OSI 7-layer model rather than TCP/IP?”

    I would say No for the following reasons: Although both models have their advantages and disadvantages, the TCP/IP model, through a 3-way handshake mechanism ensures reliable and secure connection for data transmission in a connection-oriented network (afteracademy, 2020). The OSI model on the other hand has no special way to ensure this secure and reliable connection.TCP/IP model is older than the OSI and is well established throughout the world (Frenzel,2013).
